FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is a Vision?
A Vision is a goal with a deadline. You tell Envision what you want to achieve and when — like studying for an exam or training for a race — and it automatically breaks the goal into daily tasks so you always know what to do today.
What's the difference between a Vision and a Rhythm?
A Vision has a finish line — it ends when you reach the deadline. A Rhythm is an ongoing habit you want to build steadily over time, like a weekly pushup target or daily reading, with no end date.
What is a Memory Hook?
A Memory Hook is a short note — often just one sentence — about your day, attached to that date on your calendar. It's a lightweight way to remember not just what you did, but who you were that day. You can attach photos too.
Where is my data stored?
Your data is stored locally on your device. We don't sell it or share it. See our Privacy Policy below for full details.
What happens if I miss a day of a Vision?
Nothing bad — that's the whole point. Envision automatically recalculates and redistributes your remaining tasks across the days you have left, so you stay on track without the guilt.
How do I delete my data?
Because your data lives on your device, deleting the app removes your data. You can also delete individual Visions, Rhythms, and Memory Hooks at any time within the app.
Do you have an Android version?
Not yet — Envision is currently iPhone-only. Android is on our roadmap for the future.
Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Envision ("we," "our," or "the app") is built with your privacy as a priority. This policy explains what data the app handles and how. By using Envision, you agree to the practices described here.

The short version

Envision stores the information you create — your Visions, Rhythms, to-dos, Memory Hooks, and any photos you attach — locally on your device. We do not sell your data, we do not share it with advertisers, and we do not maintain a copy of your personal content on our own servers.

Information stored on your device

When you use Envision, the following is saved locally on your iPhone so the app can function:

  • Goals and tasks you create (Visions, Rhythms, To-Dos, Schedule Blocks)
  • Journal entries and notes (Memory Hooks)
  • Photos you choose to attach to Memory Hooks
  • Your in-app preferences and settings

This information stays on your device. If you delete the app, this data is removed.

Calendar access

If you choose to connect Apple Calendar, Envision accesses your calendar events so it can display them alongside your tasks and find open time for scheduling. This calendar information is used only within the app on your device to power these features. We do not upload, store, or share your calendar data on our servers. You can revoke calendar access at any time in your device's Settings.

Notifications

If you enable reminders, Envision uses your device's notification system to alert you. You can turn notifications on or off at any time in your device settings.

Photos

If you choose to attach a photo to a Memory Hook, the app accesses your photo library only to let you select that image. The photo is stored within the app on your device. We do not upload your photos to any server.

Analytics and third parties

The app may use Apple's standard, privacy-preserving tools (such as basic crash reporting through App Store Connect) to help us fix bugs and improve stability. This information is aggregated and does not identify you personally. We do not use third-party advertising or tracking services.

Children's privacy

Envision is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

As Envision adds new features — such as cloud sync, account creation, or AI-powered planning — this policy will be updated to reflect any changes in how data is handled, and the "last updated" date above will change accordingly. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
